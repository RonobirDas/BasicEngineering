# [Basic Engineering](index.html) -------------- # Prototyping Methods So there are a few ways to do proper circuit prototyping preboard design. I'm a big fan of first doing almost a hand layout, *then* moving onto a board layout. But for designs higher than propabaly a few MHz, low noise applications or high power, you run the risk of poor or nill circuit performance on a breadboard. [Actually just read StackExchange](https://electronics.stackexchange.com/questions/2103/when-to-avoid-using-a-breadboard) Whaddya do? Well the RF guys have known about this for a long time. But I'm sure for the past two decades the digital guys have too. The answer is obvious. Copper. Copper is your friend, your good buddy, your pal. I can suggest a pretty easy way to "simulate" a 4 layer board (PWR, GND, S1, S2). Get a (2) dual layer copper clad boards, or copper sheets + perf/vectorboard of your choice also dual layer. Make a "via" to Ground and Power. Obviously there are a lot of issues with matching, but those exist in PCBs as well. And this isn't going to help you for your fancy spectrum analyzer using some ultrasensitive ADC to measure VHF waves, but it'll probably help extend a breadboards few MHz limitation into the 10's of MHz, and probably 100's of MHz with proper filtering and matching techniques. Remeber, the more Tx Line theory and passive microwave theory you understand, the more capable of a prototype you'll be able to get.
